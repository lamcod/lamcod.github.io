Qualities

Resilience is my most valuable quality, and it has revolutionized my academic career. I can just imagine myself in the middle of a challenging semester trying to manage my workload and it feels almost overwhelming. It might be tough to balance the strain of challenging homework with personal difficulties, but this is where my resilience shines. Instead of giving in to discouragement when I face setbacks or obstacles, I see them as opportunities for growth. These low marks are not barriers to my growth but I see them as small stepping stones to my success. This mindset allows me to bounce back stronger than ever. I have learnt to ask for assistance when I need it to modify my study methods and put out the effort necessary to fully comprehend the content. During my first year of college, this trait really came forth. I was really stressed out, especially with the programming worksheet which sometimes gave me problems with the programs and I was there for hours trying to fix everything. It would have been easy to give up or take the easy way out, but my resilience wouldn't allow it. I just ask for help when needed and try my best to come up with solutions to my problems.

Thoughts

In David Keirsey's temperament theory, the Guardian temperament is characterized by traits such as strong duty, practicality, and a focus on maintaining order and stability. Guardians are known for their reliability and sense of responsibility, taking commitments seriously in both personal and professional realms. They tend to be rule-followers who value tradition and respect established norms. Practical problem-solvers, they prefer realistic and workable solutions. Stability and security are paramount to Guardians, making them uncomfortable with sudden changes. They excel in attention to detail and often contribute to their communities, valuing a structured and dependable environment while respecting authority figures.
